Help yourself first

Handy resources from the crew.

A few things worth knowing before you ever need us — starting with the five minutes that matter most.

Save this one

Burst pipe? Your first 5 minutes.

  1. 1

    Close the main water valve. Usually at your water meter, near the street boundary or garage.

  2. 2

    Switch off the geyser at the electrical board — a geyser heating an empty tank is a hazard.

  3. 3

    Open the lowest tap in the house to drain pressure out of the pipes.

  4. 4

    Move valuables clear and put a bucket under active drips.

  5. 5

    Call us on 071 793 3414. We'll talk you through the rest while the van is on its way.

Save our number: 071 793 3414

Geysers

5 signs your geyser is about to fail

Rusty water, popping sounds, or a warm dripping overflow pipe are your early warnings. Catch it before it bursts and you'll save the ceiling.

Drains

Blocked drain? Try this first

Boiling water, then a plunger, then stop — chemical cleaners damage old Cape Town pipes and make our job (and your bill) bigger.

Winter

Cold snaps burst old pipes

Lag exposed pipes before July, and know where your main valve is. Ten minutes of prep saves a flooded passage at 2am.
